# Phase-Gated Corridor Activation Criteria — Dangote Global Export Architecture

## Summary
Corridor opportunity remains valid but should be positioned as Phase-2 export architecture. Immediate posture shifts from export volume capture to activation-condition design.

## Observations
- Dangote public stance: domestic supply insulation is immediate priority.
- Export behavior has started, but likely opportunistic while domestic stabilization remains binding.
- Global crude and trade integration is already active, supporting future corridor viability.

## Assumptions
- Domestic-priority policy pressure persists near term.
- Export allocations scale only after stable refinery operations and local market coverage.
- UAE remains preferred neutral routing and aggregation zone for Phase-2 global distribution.

## Activation Gates

### Gate A — Domestic Stabilization
1. Sustained domestic supply consistency (no recurring emergency diversion patterns)
2. Stable refinery run-rate cadence
3. Predictable internal allocation framework between domestic and export channels

### Gate B — Export Allocation Readiness
4. Repeated export cargo cadence over consecutive periods
5. Named buyer/offtaker pipeline with preliminary term alignment
6. Distribution partner readiness across storage, shipping, and routing

### Gate C — Financial & Legal Bankability
7. Trade-finance approvals (inventory + cargo lines)
8. Corridor operating/legal structure cleared in UAE vehicle
9. Risk-sharing model approved (offtake/FX/political risk transfer clarity)

### Gate D — Corridor Go-Live
10. Formal SPV/JV execution with role-defined governance
11. Anchor sponsor commitments activated
12. Multi-year offtake framework contracted
