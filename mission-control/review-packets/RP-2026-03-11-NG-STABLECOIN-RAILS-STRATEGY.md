## Executive Memo (Short Form)

### Thesis
Nigeria is not merely “studying crypto.” It is moving toward a **hybrid settlement architecture** where regulated institutions, policy agencies, and stablecoin rails converge. This is a potential infrastructure shift with direct implications for commodity trade, remittance flows, and dollar-liquidity access.

### Why now
- Multi-agency policy attention indicates institutional intent to shape, not suppress, stablecoin behavior.
- Market behavior suggests stablecoins are already used as practical rails for FX protection, cross-border settlement, and low-cost transfer.
- Early integration participants can become structural chokepoints (compliance + settlement + banking interfaces).

### Strategic implication for USG
USG should prioritize becoming an architect/operator at the interface of:
1) regulated bank on/off ramps,
2) compliant programmable settlement,
3) trade and commodity corridor payment orchestration.
